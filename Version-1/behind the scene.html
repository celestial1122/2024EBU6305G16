Unraveling the Peculiarities of Miss Peregrine’s Home

If you’ve seen Miss Peregrine’s Home for Peculiar Children, you’re well aware of the extraordinary abilities that set the peculiar children apart from others. From a boy who exhales bees to a girl with a monstrous, tooth-lined maw hidden in her neck, these peculiarities are brought to life through astounding visual effects. Green screens and CGI effects were used extensively throughout the film, creating a unique and mesmerizing experience for audiences.

Let’s take a closer look at some behind-the-scenes images that reveal the magic behind this peculiar film.

Costume Design

Colleen Atwood's costume design beautifully marries the stern elegance of Victorian fashion with the fantastical elements of the peculiar children, maintaining historical accuracy with 1943 period attire. Her work with Eva Green’s costumes stands out for their lavish yet austere beauty, embodying whimsy and tradition.

The Film's Music

For the film's music, Michael Higham, transitioning from music editor to composer, collaborates with Matthew Margeson to craft a score that traverses time, encapsulating the peculiars' wonder. Their composition masterfully blends adventure, excitement, and suspense, aligning perfectly with the film's narrative and visual storytelling, marking a high point in Tim Burton's cinematic scores.
